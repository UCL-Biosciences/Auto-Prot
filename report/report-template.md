# Protein Abundance Exploratory Analysis
Outputs generated by jdgilbert245's [pipeline](https://github.com/jdgilbert245/Auto-Prot) using protein abundance data. If you have any questions or problems, please get in touch via the details on the github repo!

Some of the questions we address are:

* Are replicates more similar to one another than to other samples?
* How similar are all samples? Is there a treatment effect?
* Which proteins are differentially expressed between treatments?
* What biological pathways are differentially expressed proteins associated with?

Read on to see some common proteomics outputs,  including:

* A summary of samples and experimental design
* Clustering (PCA, NMDS)
* Hierarchical clustering (dendrogram with heatmap)
* Log fold changes of protein abundance between treatments (volcano plot)
* Table of differentially expressed proteins, associated genes, LFC and corrected p value
* Pathway/functional enrichment

## Analysis

### Clustering
We start with simple clustering. Which samples are most similar?

#### PCA Plot
PCA plots represent the distances among samples. This is a good chance to start exploring your data, looking both for interesting results and anything that suggests there is something unusual in the data generated. Does the plot below show what you expected? If there are technical or biological replicates, we generally expect them to be tightly clustered. Is that the case? What about the clustering by treatment, is that what you expected?

#### MDS Clustering
A multidimensional scaling (MDS) plot is a visualisation tool used to assess the similarity between samples. While similar to principal component analysis (PCA), MDS offers greater flexibility as it does not rely on the assumption of linearity. Instead, MDS operates on ranks, making it suitable for a wider range of data types.

Compare the MDS plot with the PCA plot above. Do they show consistent patterns in sample similarity?

### Differential Protein Abundance
Now, we look at the protein abundance and whether there are differences between treatment groups.

#### Volcano Plot
Volcano plots highlight differential protein abundance between two conditions, with log2 fold change on the x-axis and -log10 (FDR-corrected) p-value on the y-axis. Proteins that are more upregulated or downregulated are displayed as points further from the center, with blue points representing proteins with an (FDR-corrected) p-value less than $FDR_THRESHOLD. Verify data quality by ensuring symmetrical distribution and expected patterns (e.g., balanced up- and down-regulated proteins). Focus on outliers that may represent biologically meaningful changes. Use this plot to quickly identify key proteins of interest for further analysis or validation.
